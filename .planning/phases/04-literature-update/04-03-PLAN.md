\subsection{SAM3: Unified Image and Video Segmentation}

The Segment Anything Model 3 (SAM3), released in early 2026, represents a significant advancement in foundation models for computer vision \cite{kirillov2026sam3}. Unlike its predecessors limited to static images, SAM3 introduces a unified architecture capable of processing both images and video sequences through a single model.

\textbf{Architecture and Capabilities.}
SAM3 combines a DETR-based detector with an enhanced SAM 2 tracker, totaling 848 million parameters \cite{kirillov2026sam3}. The model supports three prompting modes: text prompts, visual prompts, and example-based prompts, enabling flexible zero-shot segmentation across diverse scenarios.

\textbf{Maritime Applications.}
For polar navigation, SAM3's video segmentation capability is particularly relevant. Sea ice monitoring often involves sequential imagery from moving vessels or drones, where temporal consistency in segmentation is crucial. SAM3's unified approach eliminates the need for separate image and video processing pipelines, potentially simplifying real-time ice monitoring systems.

\textbf{Integration with This Work.}
In this project, SAM3 is deployed for instance segmentation of sea ice in simulated Arctic environments (Section 3.4.1). The model's zero-shot capability enables segmentation without task-specific training, making it suitable for diverse ice conditions encountered in polar navigation scenarios.
```

\subsection{Depth Anything V3: Joint Depth and Pose Estimation}

Depth Anything V3 (DA3), published in December 2025, extends monocular depth estimation to joint prediction of depth, camera pose, and 3D Gaussian Splatting parameters \cite{zhang2025depthanything3}. This unified approach addresses the traditional separation between depth estimation and structure-from-motion pipelines.

\textbf{Technical Innovations.}
DA3 introduces several architectural improvements over its predecessor. The model employs a simplified plain transformer architecture based on DINOv2, replacing complex multi-task learning heads \cite{zhang2025depthanything3}. Key capabilities include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Multi-view joint processing for spatially consistent geometry
    \item Direct camera pose prediction without known camera parameters
    \item 3D Gaussian Splatting parameter output for neural rendering
    \item Depth-ray representation for unified geometric prediction
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Performance Metrics.}
Compared to VGGT, DA3 achieves 44.3\% improvement in camera pose accuracy and 25.1\% better geometric precision \cite{zhang2025depthanything3}. The model is available in multiple configurations, from 0.08B parameter base models to 1.4B parameter nested variants supporting metric depth and 3D Gaussian output.

\textbf{Practical Limitations.}
Despite impressive benchmark results, DA3's pose prediction exhibits limitations in specific application contexts. As detailed in Section 4.5.2, empirical evaluation revealed that DA3's predicted poses were unsuitable for this project's coordinate system requirements, necessitating fallback to simulation-provided ground truth poses. This finding highlights the importance of domain-specific validation of foundation model outputs.
```
